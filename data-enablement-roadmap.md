# Data Governance Maturity Roadmap for Utility Companies
## 3-Year Strategic Plan (2026-2028)

---

## Executive Summary

This roadmap provides a practical, phased approach to maturing data governance capabilities over three years. It is designed specifically for utility organizations navigating complex regulatory requirements while managing legacy systems and limited resources. The roadmap emphasizes quick wins, sustainable practices, and incremental capability building rather than disruptive transformation.

**Key Principles:**
- Start with high-impact, high-visibility data domains (customer, outage, safety, financial)
- Build on existing organizational structures and processes
- Demonstrate value early to secure ongoing executive support
- Balance regulatory compliance needs with operational excellence
- Create sustainable practices that don't require continuous consulting support

---

## Current State Assumptions

This roadmap assumes your utility is at an **early to intermediate** maturity level characterized by:
- Limited formal data governance structures (perhaps informal data stewards)
- Reactive approach to data quality issues
- Inconsistent data definitions across systems
- Basic security controls but limited data classification
- Compliance-driven data management practices
- Siloed data ownership across departments
- Limited enterprise view of data assets
- Minimal data literacy outside IT and analytics teams

## Year 1: Foundation & Quick Wins
### Theme: "Establish Governance Foundation and Demonstrate Value"

### Q1: Assessment, Leadership Alignment & Governance Framework

#### Milestones
- **Data Governance Charter Approved** (Month 1)
  - Secure executive sponsor (ideally CFO, COO, or CIO)
  - Define governance scope and authority
  - Establish governance operating model (council structure)

- **Critical Data Domains Identified** (Month 2)
  - Conduct rapid assessment of data landscape
  - Prioritize 3-5 critical domains (recommend: Customer, Outage, Asset/Work, Financial, Safety)
  - Document current pain points and regulatory requirements for each domain

- **Governance Council Established** (Month 3)
  - Form Data Governance Steering Committee (executive-level, meets monthly)
  - Establish Data Governance Working Group (operational-level, meets bi-weekly)
  - Appoint interim data stewards for critical domains
  - Define decision-making processes and escalation paths

#### Deliverables
- Data Governance Charter (2-3 pages)
- Governance Operating Model diagram with roles/responsibilities
- Critical Data Domain Assessment Report
- Meeting cadence and agenda templates
- Initial governance roadmap communication to organization

#### Success Metrics
- Executive sponsor identified and actively engaged
- Steering committee attendance >80%
- Critical domains agreed upon by business leadership

---

### Q2: Data Quality Baseline & Policy Foundation

#### Milestones
- **Data Quality Baseline Established for Priority Domain** (Month 4-5)
  - Select one high-pain domain (typically Customer or Outage data)
  - Define 5-10 critical data quality rules
  - Measure current quality levels
  - Identify top 3 root causes of quality issues

- **Initial Data Classification Framework** (Month 5)
  - Develop 3-tier classification scheme (Public, Internal, Confidential/Critical)
  - Define handling requirements for each tier
  - Align with existing IT security policies
  - Address regulatory data categories (CUI, PII, CIP for electric utilities)

- **Core Data Policies Drafted** (Month 6)
  - Data classification policy
  - Data quality accountability policy
  - Data retention framework (align with records management)
  - Data access and sharing principles

#### Deliverables
- Data Quality Baseline Report for priority domain
- Data Classification Framework (1-2 pages)
- Draft core data policies (3-4 policies)
- Data quality dashboard prototype (can be simple Excel/Power BI)

#### Success Metrics
- Quality baseline measured with specific metrics
- Data classification framework approved by security and compliance teams
- At least one policy ready for approval

---

### Q3: Quick Wins & Data Storage Assessment

#### Milestones
- **First Data Quality Improvement Delivered** (Month 7-8)
  - Implement fixes for top 1-2 quality issues identified in Q2
  - Document process improvements
  - Measure and communicate impact (e.g., "Reduced customer address errors by 40%")

- **Data Storage Landscape Assessment** (Month 8)
  - Inventory current data storage platforms (CIS, OMS, EAM, GIS, data warehouses, cloud)
  - Assess storage costs, redundancy, and lifecycle practices
  - Identify immediate rationalization opportunities
  - Document data flows between major systems

- **Initial Data Tagging Strategy** (Month 9)
  - Define critical metadata to capture (data owner, classification, retention period, source system)
  - Select pilot system or dataset for tagging
  - Choose approach: system metadata, spreadsheet registry, or simple tool
  - Begin tagging pilot

#### Deliverables
- Data Quality Improvement Case Study (internal communication)
- Data Storage Assessment Report with cost analysis
- Data Tagging Framework and Pilot Plan
- Initial Business Glossary (10-20 critical terms for priority domain)

#### Success Metrics
- Measurable quality improvement demonstrated
- Storage assessment identifies 2-3 rationalization opportunities
- Pilot dataset tagged with critical metadata

---

### Q4: Data Literacy Launch & DLP Readiness

#### Milestones
- **Data Literacy Program Launched** (Month 10)
  - Develop 3-tier curriculum: Awareness (all staff), Practitioner (data workers), Advanced (analysts/scientists)
  - Create initial training content for Awareness tier
  - Conduct "Lunch & Learn" sessions on data governance basics
  - Establish internal SharePoint/Teams site as data governance knowledge hub

- **DLP Requirements Defined** (Month 11)
  - Conduct gap analysis of current data loss prevention capabilities
  - Define DLP scope (email, endpoints, cloud applications, removable media)
  - Prioritize sensitive data types (customer PII, operational data, financial data)
  - Develop business case for DLP tools if needed

- **Year 1 Governance Metrics Published** (Month 12)
  - Document governance activities and participation
  - Report data quality trends for priority domain
  - Survey governance participants on effectiveness
  - Present Year 1 accomplishments to executive leadership

#### Deliverables
- Data Literacy Curriculum Framework
- Awareness-level training content (slides, videos, quick reference guides)
- DLP Requirements Document and Gap Analysis
- Year 1 Governance Annual Report

#### Success Metrics
- 50% of organization completes data governance awareness training
- DLP requirements defined and approved
- Positive feedback from governance council participants
- Executive sponsor commits to Year 2 funding

## Year 2: Scale & Standardization
### Theme: "Expand Coverage and Embed Governance Practices"

### Q1: Expand Data Stewardship & Storage Optimization

#### Milestones
- **Formalized Data Stewardship Model** (Month 13-14)
  - Convert interim stewards to formal role assignments
  - Develop steward job descriptions and success criteria
  - Establish steward community of practice (monthly meetings)
  - Create steward toolkit (templates, playbooks, escalation guides)
  - Expand stewardship to 2-3 additional data domains

- **Data Storage Optimization Initiated** (Month 14-15)
  - Implement 1-2 quick wins from Year 1 assessment (e.g., decommission redundant databases)
  - Develop enterprise data storage standards
  - Define cloud vs. on-premise criteria for new data assets
  - Establish data archival process for dormant data

#### Deliverables
- Data Steward Charter and Toolkit
- Steward assignments for 5-8 critical domains
- Data Storage Standards Document
- Storage optimization plan with ROI analysis

#### Success Metrics
- 5-8 active data stewards with defined responsibilities
- At least one storage system decommissioned or consolidated
- Documented storage cost savings

### Q2: DLP Implementation & Enhanced Data Quality

#### Milestones
- **DLP Tool Deployment (Phase 1)** (Month 16-18)
  - Deploy DLP for email and endpoint (if new tool required)
  - Configure rules for top-priority data types (SSN, credit card, CEII for electric utilities)
  - Implement monitoring and alerting
  - Train security team on DLP operations
  - Begin user awareness campaign on data handling

- **Data Quality Expanded to Additional Domains** (Month 17-18)
  - Apply data quality framework to 2 additional domains
  - Establish data quality scorecards
  - Implement automated quality monitoring where feasible
  - Assign quality issue resolution workflows

#### Deliverables
- DLP System operational for email and endpoints
- DLP Policy Documentation and User Guidance
- Data Quality Scorecards for 3 critical domains
- Quarterly Data Quality Review Process

#### Success Metrics
- DLP rules operational with <5% false positive rate
- Data quality metrics tracked for 3 domains
- Quality improvement trends visible in at least 2 domains

### Q3: Advanced Tagging & Master Data Foundation

#### Milestones
- **Enterprise Data Catalog Established** (Month 19-20)
  - Select and implement data catalog tool (consider utility-friendly options: Collibra, Alation, or lighter-weight alternatives)
  - Migrate tagging pilot to catalog platform
  - Onboard 3-5 critical source systems
  - Establish metadata curation process

- **Master Data Management (MDM) Strategy** (Month 20-21)
  - Assess readiness for MDM in 1-2 domains (typically Customer and/or Asset)
  - Define golden record requirements
  - Select MDM approach (tool-based vs. process-based)
  - Develop MDM roadmap
  - Begin MDM pilot if feasible (or defer to Year 3)

#### Deliverables
- Data Catalog populated with critical datasets
- Metadata Standards and Curation Process
- Business Glossary expanded to 100+ terms
- MDM Strategy and Business Case

#### Success Metrics
- 80% of critical datasets cataloged with metadata
- Users can discover and understand key datasets via catalog
- MDM approach approved by governance council

## Cross-Cutting Initiatives (All Years)

### Regulatory Compliance Integration
- **Year 1:** Map data governance to existing compliance obligations (financial reporting, safety, environmental)
- **Year 2:** Establish compliance reporting from governed data; reduce manual effort
- **Year 3:** Proactive compliance monitoring; governance as competitive advantage in rate cases

### Change Management & Communication
- **Year 1:** Executive sponsorship; governance council engagement; quick win communication
- **Year 2:** Expanded stakeholder engagement; success stories; recognition programs
- **Year 3:** Governance embedded in culture; continuous feedback loops; knowledge sharing

### Technology Enablement
- **Year 1:** Leverage existing tools (Excel, Power BI, SharePoint); minimal new investment
- **Year 2:** Targeted tool acquisition (DLP, data catalog); integration with existing platforms
- **Year 3:** Advanced tools as justified by value delivered (MDM, automated quality, ML-powered catalog)

## Dependencies & Sequencing

### Critical Path Dependencies
1. **Governance Foundation → All Other Initiatives**: Executive sponsorship and governance structure must be in place before scaling efforts
2. **Data Classification → DLP**: Cannot effectively implement DLP without understanding what data to protect
3. **Data Quality Baseline → Data Literacy**: Need concrete examples and metrics to make literacy training relevant
4. **Data Catalog → Self-Service Analytics**: Users need to discover and understand data before self-service is effective
5. **Stewardship Model → MDM**: MDM requires active stewards to manage master data

### Recommended Sequencing Rationale
- **Year 1** focuses on governance structure, quick wins, and assessments to build credibility and understanding
- **Year 2** scales proven practices and implements foundational technologies (DLP, catalog)
- **Year 3** optimizes and enables advanced capabilities once foundation is solid

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Common Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Loss of executive sponsor | Medium | High | Engage multiple executive stakeholders; demonstrate ROI early; tie to regulatory compliance |
| Resistance from data owners/stewards | High | Medium | Start with willing participants; show value through quick wins; provide adequate support and tools |
| Insufficient resources | High | High | Phase initiatives; leverage existing resources; build business case with cost savings and risk reduction |
| Tool selection paralysis | Medium | Medium | Start with simple tools; define requirements based on actual needs; pilot before enterprise deployment |
| Governance seen as bureaucracy | High | Medium | Streamline processes; focus on enabling rather than controlling; communicate value stories |
| Data quality issues overwhelming | Medium | Medium | Prioritize critical domains; focus on root causes; celebrate incremental improvement |
| Regulatory changes | Medium | Medium | Build flexible frameworks; maintain regulatory awareness; participate in industry forums |
| Cybersecurity incidents | Low | Very High | Integrate governance with security; accelerate DLP deployment; maintain incident response integration |

## Utility-Specific Considerations

### Regulatory Alignment
- **Electric Utilities:** Align data classification with NERC CIP requirements for BES Cyber System Information; ensure outage and reliability data governance supports FERC/state reporting
- **Gas Utilities:** Governance for pipeline safety data (PHMSA compliance); leak detection and repair data quality
- **Water Utilities:** Water quality data governance (EPA compliance); SDWA reporting reliability
- **All Utilities:** Customer data privacy (state PUC requirements); financial data for rate cases; asset data for capital planning

### Operational Integration
- Integrate governance into existing operational processes (outage management, work management, customer service)
- Ensure governance doesn't impede 24/7 operational response
- Consider shift worker access to training and governance tools
- Align data retention with operational technology refresh cycles

### Union Considerations (if applicable)
- Involve union representatives in governance council if data impacts workforce
- Ensure training is accessible to all employee groups
- Consider data governance roles as developmental opportunities
- Address workforce data privacy concerns proactively

## Customization Guidance

This roadmap is designed as a starting point. Customize based on:

### Organizational Context
- **Smaller utilities (<500 employees):** Compress timeline; combine roles; leverage cloud tools to minimize infrastructure
- **Larger utilities (>2000 employees):** Expand scope; add specialized roles; consider more sophisticated tooling earlier
- **Multi-utility holding companies:** Add governance layer for cross-utility data sharing and standards

### Maturity Starting Point
- **Very immature (no governance):** Spend extra time in Year 1 on education and culture change
- **Intermediate (some governance):** Accelerate through Year 1; build on existing structures
- **Domain-specific maturity:** Some areas may progress faster (e.g., strong financial data governance but weak operational data governance)

### Strategic Drivers
- **Regulatory pressure:** Accelerate DLP and compliance-related initiatives
- **Digital transformation:** Emphasize data catalog and self-service analytics earlier
- **Merger/acquisition activity:** Prioritize data standards and MDM
- **Grid modernization/AMI:** Focus on operational data governance and real-time data quality
- **Customer experience initiatives:** Emphasize customer data quality and privacy

## Appendix B: Quick Win Ideas for Year 1

*Select 2-3 of these based on your organization's pain points:*

1. **Customer Address Standardization:** Clean and standardize customer addresses; immediate benefits for service delivery and billing
2. **Outage Cause Code Consistency:** Standardize outage cause codes; improves reliability reporting and root cause analysis
3. **Asset Hierarchy Cleanup:** Fix parent-child relationships in asset master data; enables better work planning and capital budgeting
4. **Duplicate Customer Cleanup:** Identify and merge duplicate customer records; improves customer experience and billing accuracy
5. **Data Access Request Tracking:** Implement simple process to track and fulfill data requests; demonstrates governance value
6. **Critical Data Element Inventory:** Identify and document the 50-100 most critical data elements; foundation for all other work
7. **Regulatory Reporting Data Quality:** Improve quality of data feeding a specific regulatory report; reduces audit findings
8. **Vendor Data Cleanup:** Standardize vendor master data; supports procurement efficiency and spend analysis

## Appendix C: Sample Data Steward Role Description

**Position Title:** Data Steward (Domain-Specific)

**Time Commitment:** 15-25% of role (approximately 6-10 hours per week)

**Reporting:** Functionally reports to Data Governance Lead; administratively to current manager

**Purpose:** Ensure [specific data domain] is accurate, accessible, and appropriately used across the organization

**Key Responsibilities:**
1. Define and maintain business definitions for domain data elements
2. Establish and monitor data quality rules and metrics
3. Investigate and resolve data quality issues
4. Participate in governance working group meetings
5. Review and approve data access requests for domain data
6. Support data literacy and training for domain data users
7. Represent business perspective in system implementations affecting domain data
8. Identify opportunities for data-driven improvement in domain processes

**Qualifications:**
- Subject matter expert in domain business processes
- 3+ years experience in domain area
- Strong communication and collaboration skills
- Basic understanding of data and systems
- Commitment to data quality and governance principles

**Support Provided:**
- Data governance training and certification
- Steward toolkit (templates, processes, playbooks)
- Community of practice with other stewards
- Governance Lead support for escalations and technical questions
- Recognition in performance review process

## Appendix D: Glossary of Terms

**Business Glossary:** A catalog of business terms with definitions, synonyms, and relationships; helps ensure common understanding across organization

**Data Catalog:** A metadata repository that helps users discover, understand, and access data assets

**Data Classification:** The process of categorizing data based on sensitivity, criticality, and regulatory requirements

**Data Custodian:** The role (typically IT) responsible for technical storage, security, and maintenance of data

**Data Domain:** A subject area of data (e.g., Customer, Asset, Outage, Financial) with related business meaning

**Data Governance:** The organizational framework for managing data as an asset, including policies, processes, roles, and standards

**Data Lineage:** Documentation of data flow from source systems through transformations to consumption

**Data Loss Prevention (DLP):** Technologies and processes to detect and prevent unauthorized data sharing or exfiltration

**Data Owner:** The business role accountable for a data domain, with authority to make decisions about access, quality standards, and usage

**Data Quality:** Fitness of data for intended use; typically measured across dimensions like accuracy, completeness, timeliness, consistency

**Data Steward:** The operational role responsible for day-to-day data quality, definitions, and issue resolution within a domain

**Golden Record:** The single, authoritative version of a data entity (e.g., the definitive customer record) in Master Data Management

**Master Data:** Critical business entities (customers, assets, locations, products) that are shared across systems and processes

**Master Data Management (MDM):** Processes and technologies to create and maintain authoritative, consistent master data

**Metadata:** "Data about data" - information describing data assets, such as source, owner, definition, quality metrics, lineage

## Conclusion

This roadmap provides a realistic path for maturing data governance capabilities over three years. Success requires:

1. **Executive commitment:** Sustained sponsorship and resources
2. **Incremental approach:** Build on successes rather than attempting transformation
3. **Value demonstration:** Regular communication of business benefits achieved
4. **Cultural change:** Embedding data stewardship into roles and processes
5. **Practical tools:** Right-sized technology investments based on demonstrated need
6. **Continuous improvement:** Regular assessment and adaptation of governance practices

Remember that data governance is a journey, not a destination. The specific initiatives and timeline should be adapted to your utility's unique context, regulatory environment, and strategic priorities. The key is to start, demonstrate value, and build sustainable practices that mature over time.

**Questions or need to adapt this roadmap? Consider:**
- What are your top 3 data pain points today?
- What regulatory or business drivers create urgency?
- What governance elements already exist to build upon?
- What resources (people, budget) are realistically available?
- What does success look like in 12 months? 24 months? 36 months?

Use this roadmap as a conversation starter with your governance team and executives to build a shared vision for data governance maturity that fits your organization's needs and capabilities.
